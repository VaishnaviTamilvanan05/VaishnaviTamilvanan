Washington State Electric Vehicle Adoption & Charging Infrastructure Expansion

This project addresses the growing need to align electric vehicle (EV) adoption trends with charging infrastructure development in Washington State through 2028. Historical EV registration data and charging station locations were collected from the Washington Open Data Portal and OpenChargeMap. After data cleaning, time indexing, and aggregation at county and ZIP-code levels, advanced forecasting models—including ARIMA, SARIMA, and Prophet with external regressors (average EV sale price)—were applied to project monthly and annual EV growth across the top 10 counties. Charging infrastructure analysis was conducted using DOE/NREL benchmarks to calculate EV-to-station ratios and identify regional capacity gaps. A machine learning regression model was used to predict annual charging station needs (2025–2028), and underserved ZIP codes were prioritized using normalized demand-supply scores. All insights were consolidated into an interactive Power BI dashboard with downloadable reports, empowering state planners and policymakers to make data-driven decisions on equitable and scalable EV infrastructure deployment.
